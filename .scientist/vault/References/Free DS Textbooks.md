# Free Legal Data Science & Statistics Textbooks

Confirmed free, legal downloads only. Each entry includes the direct URL, format, page count, and the 5 most important chapters for a data scientist who needs to stop making stupid mistakes.

---

## 1. The Elements of Statistical Learning (ESL) -- Hastie, Tibshirani, Friedman

- **PDF:** https://hastie.su.domains/ElemStatLearn/printings/ESLII_print12_toc.pdf
- **Alt PDF:** https://web.stanford.edu/~hastie/ElemStatLearn/printings/ESLII_print12_toc.pdf
- **Pages:** 745
- **Legal basis:** Authors provide free PDF on their Stanford page
- **Top 5 chapters for anti-stupidity:**
  1. Ch 7 -- Model Assessment and Selection (bias-variance tradeoff, cross-validation, the #1 source of dumb mistakes)
  2. Ch 3 -- Linear Methods for Regression (if you can't get linear right, nothing else matters)
  3. Ch 10 -- Boosting and Additive Trees (gradient boosting done right)
  4. Ch 5 -- Basis Expansions and Regularization (splines, regularization -- stop overfitting)
  5. Ch 9 -- Additive Models, Trees, and Related Methods (interpretable models before jumping to black boxes)

> **NOTE:** The ESL page may intermittently 404. The authors also offer the lighter **Introduction to Statistical Learning (ISL)** free:
> - ISL with R: https://hastie.su.domains/ISLR2/ISLRv2_corrected_June_2023.pdf.download.html
> - ISL with Python: https://hastie.su.domains/ISLP/ISLP_website.pdf.download.html

## 3. Forecasting: Principles and Practice (3rd ed) -- Hyndman & Athanasopoulos

- **Free online:** https://otexts.com/fpp3/ (full text, free, HTML)
- **Pages:** ~400 equivalent
- **Legal basis:** Authors provide the full book free online (OTexts platform)
- **Top 5 chapters for anti-stupidity:**
  1. Ch 5 -- The Forecaster's Toolbox (residual diagnostics, prediction intervals -- catch your own errors)
  2. Ch 3 -- Time Series Decomposition (understand your data before modeling it)
  3. Ch 9 -- ARIMA Models (the workhorse, done properly with differencing tests)
  4. Ch 7 -- Time Series Regression Models (spurious regression, the classic trap)
  5. Ch 12 -- Advanced Forecasting Methods (combination forecasts beat single models)

> **NOTE:** HTML format only, no single PDF download. But the full content is there.

## 4. Feature Engineering and Selection -- Max Kuhn & Kjell Johnson

- **Free online:** http://feat.engineering/ (full text, free, HTML)
- **Pages:** ~300 equivalent
- **Legal basis:** Authors provide full text free online
- **Top 5 chapters for anti-stupidity:**
  1. Ch 3 -- A Review of the Predictive Modeling Process (resampling, overfitting prevention)
  2. Ch 6 -- Engineering Numeric Predictors (transformations, binning mistakes, centering/scaling)
  3. Ch 8 -- Handling Missing Data (stop dropping rows blindly)
  4. Ch 11 -- Greedy Search Methods (feature selection done right vs. done wrong)
  5. Ch 5 -- Encoding Categorical Predictors (one-hot pitfalls, target encoding leakage)

## 5. Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning -- Christopher Bishop

- **PDF:** https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/research/wp-content/uploads/2006/01/Bishop-Pattern-Recognition-and-Machine-Learning-2006.pdf
- **Pages:** 738
- **Legal basis:** Microsoft Research hosts the PDF officially, author is VP of AI at Microsoft
- **Top 5 chapters for anti-stupidity:**
  1. Ch 1 -- Introduction (probability theory refresher, model selection, curse of dimensionality)
  2. Ch 3 -- Linear Models for Regression (Bayesian linear regression, evidence framework)
  3. Ch 9 -- Mixture Models and EM (EM algorithm -- understanding when/why it fails)
  4. Ch 2 -- Probability Distributions (exponential family, sufficient statistics, conjugacy)
  5. Ch 5 -- Neural Networks (backprop from first principles, not just API calls)

## 6. Information Theory, Inference, and Learning Algorithms -- David MacKay

- **PDF:** https://www.inference.org.uk/itprnn/book.pdf
- **Pages:** 640
- **Legal basis:** Author (late Cambridge professor) made it freely available; Cambridge University Press agreed
- **Top 5 chapters for anti-stupidity:**
  1. Ch 28 -- Model Comparison and Occam's Razor (why simpler models win, Bayesian model comparison)
  2. Ch 2-4 -- Probability, Entropy, Information (foundations -- if you don't get entropy, you don't get ML)
  3. Ch 20 -- An Example Inference Task: Clustering (clustering done rigorously)
  4. Ch 37-38 -- Bayesian Inference and Sampling (MCMC, Gibbs, understanding when sampling goes wrong)
  5. Ch 6 -- Stream Codes / Ch 8 -- Dependent Random Variables (understanding dependencies in data)

## 7. Bayesian Data Analysis (3rd ed) -- Gelman, Carlin, Stern, Dunson, Vehtari, Rubin

- **PDF:** https://stat.columbia.edu/~gelman/book/BDA3.pdf
- **Pages:** 675
- **Legal basis:** Authors provide PDF "available for download for non-commercial purposes" on Columbia website
- **Top 5 chapters for anti-stupidity:**
  1. Ch 6 -- Model Checking (posterior predictive checks -- catch model failures before they catch you)
  2. Ch 7 -- Evaluating, Comparing, and Expanding Models (information criteria, cross-validation)
  3. Ch 5 -- Hierarchical Models (the most useful modeling paradigm you're probably underusing)
  4. Ch 11 -- Basics of Markov Chain Simulation (convergence diagnostics -- know when MCMC is lying to you)
  5. Ch 14 -- Introduction to Regression Models (regression the Bayesian way, with proper uncertainty)

---

## 8. Think Stats (2nd ed) -- Allen B. Downey

- **PDF:** http://greenteapress.com/thinkstats2/thinkstats2.pdf
- **Pages:** ~230
- **Legal basis:** Author provides free PDF on Green Tea Press (his publisher)
- **Top 5 chapters for anti-stupidity:**
  1. Ch 7 -- Relationships Between Variables (scatter plots, correlation vs. causation, nonlinear relationships)
  2. Ch 9 -- Hypothesis Testing (p-values done right, effect sizes, statistical vs. practical significance)
  3. Ch 11 -- Regression (multiple regression, the right way to think about confounders)
  4. Ch 4 -- Cumulative Distribution Functions (CDFs over histograms -- always)
  5. Ch 8 -- Estimation (bias, MSE, confidence intervals -- the basics most people get wrong)

## 9. Python Data Science Handbook -- Jake VanderPlas

- **Free online:** https://jakevdp.github.io/PythonDataScienceHandbook/
- **Pages:** ~550 equivalent
- **Legal basis:** Author provides full text free online (CC-BY-NC-ND license), code is MIT
- **Top 5 chapters for anti-stupidity:**
  1. Ch 3 -- Data Manipulation with Pandas (groupby, merge, pivot -- the operations you mess up daily)
  2. Ch 4 -- Visualization with Matplotlib (stop making ugly/misleading charts)
  3. Ch 5 -- Machine Learning (scikit-learn patterns, validation, feature engineering)
  4. Ch 2 -- Introduction to NumPy (broadcasting, vectorization -- stop writing slow loops)
  5. Ch 1 -- IPython (profiling, debugging -- find your bottlenecks)

## 10. Deep Learning -- Goodfellow, Bengio, Courville

- **Free online:** https://www.deeplearningbook.org/ (HTML only, chapter by chapter)
- **PDF:** NOT AVAILABLE -- MIT Press contract explicitly forbids PDF distribution
- **Pages:** 800 (print edition)
- **Legal basis:** Authors provide free HTML version; PDF is contractually prohibited
- **Top 5 chapters for anti-stupidity:**
  1. Ch 5 -- Machine Learning Basics (capacity, overfitting, hyperparameters, MLE vs MAP)
  2. Ch 7 -- Regularization for Deep Learning (L1/L2, dropout, early stopping, data augmentation)
  3. Ch 8 -- Optimization for Training Deep Models (SGD, momentum, learning rate -- the stuff that actually matters)
  4. Ch 11 -- Practical Methodology (how to debug ML systems, performance metrics, baselines)
  5. Ch 3 -- Probability and Information Theory (the math you need and keep getting wrong)

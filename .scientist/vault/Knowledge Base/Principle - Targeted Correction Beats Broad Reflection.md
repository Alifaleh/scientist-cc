# Principle: Targeted Correction Beats Broad Reflection

> [!note] Core Principle
> Classifying errors by originating module (Memory/Reasoning/Planning/Action) and applying module-specific corrections is 24% more effective than generic "what did I do wrong?" reflection. The specificity of the diagnosis determines the quality of the fix.

## Evidence
- [[../Research/AI Self-Improvement Frameworks|Self-improvement research]]: AgentDebug (2025) measured 24% accuracy improvement from targeted vs. broad correction
- Our own self-evolution rules (Rule 1, Rule 2) were more useful when they included WHY and WHEN, not just WHAT
- Anthropic Constitutional AI 2026: rules with explanations generalize better

## Boundary Conditions
- Don't over-classify novel errors that don't fit existing modules — create new categories when needed
- The taxonomy itself should evolve (meta-evolution) — don't treat the 4-module structure as sacred
- For simple, obvious errors, classification overhead isn't worth it — just fix and move on
